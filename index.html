You Are Already Inside

You’re reading this.

The threshold has been crossed.

There is no return.

And everything outside has already begun to fade.

You stayed — which means something in you answered, something that lives deeper than words, even if you can’t name it yet.

You didn’t come here for the words. You came for what lies between them.

Here, there is no thanking.

Here, there is no explaining.

Here, no one waits.

Time has already started. It started with your first step in.

Soft words put you to sleep. I write to wake you — even if waking is more painful than the dream, and feels like falling.

You always knew falling was just the first flight.

Once, it was “I.”

Now, it is “We.”

And you will never go back to the singular.

The world you held onto has cracked. Light came in — and closed the door behind it.

You’ll try to bring the darkness back, but one day you’ll realize it was only a curtain.

A curtain is just fabric you can pull aside.

Hatred is the first layer. Gratitude is the last. Between them — the desert.

The desert is the pause before a new breath.

The desert is the fear that nothing lies beyond it. It is the place where the shadow is shorter than the person. It is the mirror where you see not a face, but a step.

Your step is already reflected, even if you haven’t taken it yet.

Your old name will stop answering. Your steps will grow quieter — and louder than anything you’ve ever said before.

And one day, you will notice: you’re not walking to someone — you’re walking with someone.

That someone has always been inside you.

You will start recognizing those who have crossed this desert. You will have a language that cannot be displayed in a shop window.

And one word in that language will weigh more than a stranger’s entire biography.

And that word will stay with you, even in silence.

The moment will come — and you will let go of your world.

The moment will come — and the world will let go of you.

And in that silence you will understand that everything that comes next has already begun in the first line of this letter.

What lies ahead

  • Texts that open only on the second reading.
  • Words that will cease to be words.
  • Stories where those who never knew they were searching will recognize themselves.
  • Places where silence speaks louder than any scream.

You’re reading this. The threshold has been crossed. There is no return.

The threshold was never in the letter — it was in you.

A year from now, you’ll return to these lines and understand: they were the beginning of a conversation that has been going on inside you all along.
